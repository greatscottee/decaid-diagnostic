Credit union consent journey map

Seven consent moments · Canadian regulatory context · Member lifecycle view

DecAid Consulting decaidconsulting.com

Consent in Canadian credit unions exists today as a series of disconnected moments — an onboarding form, a CASL checkbox, an OAuth screen — rather than a managed member lifecycle. As Consumer-Driven Banking (CDB), RTR, and CPPA converge, the gap between where most credit unions are and where they need to be is closing fast. This map identifies the seven distinct consent journeys, what each requires, and how mature the sector currently is at executing them.

The structural gap: seven journeys, zero lifecycle

The problem is not that any one of these journeys is missing — it is that they are not connected. Preferences captured at onboarding are not surfaced to advisors. CASL opt-outs do not flow to the CRM. Fintech consent screens are not linked to a member-accessible revocation dashboard. Each journey is an island.

Credit unions that build a unified consent lifecycle — preference capture → CRM sync → CDB authorization layer → RTR mandate management — arrive at RPAA and RTR compliance with infrastructure already in place, not scrambling to bolt on compliance features after the regulatory deadline passes.

Positioning
A credit union that builds a member-facing consent dashboard — one place to view, update, and revoke all authorizations — turns a compliance obligation into a visible expression of cooperative values. No chartered bank will lead here. The credit union that does becomes the most trusted financial institution in the member's life, not by assertion, but by architecture.
