This diagnostic assesses your credit union's maturity across the seven consent journeys that matter most as Consumer-Driven Banking (CDB), RTR, and CPPA converge. Each question covers a distinct regulatory obligation. Results identify your priority gaps and recommended next steps. Scores are for internal positioning use.

A
Foundational consent
Question 01 PIPEDA / CASL
How does your credit union capture member data consent at account opening?
Covers PIPEDA-required consent for collection, use and disclosure, plus CASL marketing opt-in.
Question 02 CASL s.6–8 · PIPEDA s.4.3
How do you manage member communications preferences — channel, frequency and topic?
Covers CASL compliance and the meaningful consent standard under PIPEDA s.4.3.
B
Payments & data sharing
Question 03 PIPEDA meaningful consent · CPPA
When a fintech partner accesses member data, what consent process exists?
Purpose-specific consent — distinct from membership consent — is required for each partner integration under PIPEDA's meaningful consent standard.
Question 04 RPAA / CDB framework · FCAC
What is your current Consumer-Driven Banking (CDB) and RPAA consent readiness?
CDB will require accredited third-party authorization with scope, duration and revocation — replacing screen-scraping arrangements where consent accountability is currently ambiguous.
Question 05 Payments Canada RTR · ISO 20022
For Real-Time Rail (RTR), how prepared are you for request-to-pay consent and mandate management?
Covers Confirmation of Payee (CoP) and pull-payment authorization under the Payments Canada RTR Participation Guide.
C
Emerging obligations
Question 06 PIPEDA s.4.3 · CPPA Bill C-27
When advisor interactions are recorded in your CRM and used to trigger member outreach, what consent basis applies?
Advisor-captured data used for marketing triggers requires a disclosed consent basis under PIPEDA — treating it as purely internal does not satisfy the obligation.
Question 07 CPPA Bill C-27 · AIDA (proposed)
If your credit union uses or plans to use AI for product recommendations or financial coaching, what consent framework exists?
Automated decision-making and behavioral profiling require explicit consent under CPPA and PIPEDA's meaningful consent standard. Deploying before the framework is defined creates compounding exposure.
